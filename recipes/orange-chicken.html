Orange Chicken

Orange chicken is a popular Chinese-American dish featuring crispy, breaded chicken bites coated in a tangy and sweet orange sauce. The combination of savory, sweet, and citrusy flavors makes it a favorite in takeout menus and home kitchens alike. Itâ€™s typically served over steamed rice or with vegetables for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • For the chicken:
    • 1 lb (450 g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup cornstarch
    • 2 eggs, beaten
    • Oil for frying
  • For the Orange Sauce:
    • 1/2 cup orange juice (freshly squeezed or store-bought)
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
    • 1/3 cup sugar or honey (adjust to taste)
    • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (optional)
    • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
    • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
    • 1/4 teaspoon red chili flakes (optional, for heat)
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (slurry for thickening)
  • For Garnish:
    • Sesame seeds
    • Sliced green onions

Steps

  1. Prepare the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, mix the flour and cornstarch
    • Dip each piece of chicken into the beaten eggs, then coat in the flour-cornstarch mixture. Set aside
  2. Fry the Chicken:
    • Heat oil in a deep skillet or work over medium-high heat
    • Fry the chicken pieces in batches until golden brown and crisp (about 4-5m minutes). Remove and drain on paper towels
  3. Make the Orange Sauce:
    • In a saucepan, combine orange juice, soy, sauce, sugar (or honey), rice vinegar, sesame oil (optional), garlic, ginger, and chilli flakes (if using)
    • Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves
    • Stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens
  4. Combine Chicken and Sauce:
    • Add the fried chicken to the saucepan with the orange sauce. Toss until the chicken is fully coated
  5. Serve
    • Transfer to a serving dish, garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions, and serve immediately with steamed rice or vegetable
